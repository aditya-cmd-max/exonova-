Science Facts

Astronomy

There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all Earth's beaches combined - about 70 sextillion (7×10²²) stars versus approximately 7.5×10¹⁸ grains of sand.

Physics

An atom is about 99.9999999999996% empty space. If an atom's nucleus were the size of a peanut, the electrons would orbit about a kilometer away.

Biology

The human brain generates about 12-25 watts of electricity - enough to power a low-wattage LED light bulb. It processes information at speeds up to 120 meters per second.

Genetics

If uncoiled, the DNA in all your body's cells would stretch about 10 billion miles - that's from Earth to Pluto and back several times over.

Planetary

Olympus Mons on Mars is the largest volcano in our solar system - about 370 miles in diameter (size of Arizona) and 16 miles high (3x Everest).

Astrophysics

A black hole's gravity warps spacetime so severely that not even light can escape. The largest known black hole (TON 618) has 66 billion solar masses.
