The Evolution of Google

Published on 15th June, 2025

Series: Tech Titans

What began as a research project in a Stanford dorm room has grown into the most influential tech company in history. Google's journey from Backrub to Alphabet represents one of the most remarkable transformations in business history, reshaping how humanity accesses information, communicates, and interacts with technology.

Fun Fact: The domain google.com was registered on September 15, 1997, but the company wasn't officially incorporated until September 4, 1998‚Äîin a Menlo Park garage that later became famous as Google's first office.

The Garage Days: Google's Humble Beginnings

In 1995, Larry Page was considering Stanford for grad school when he was assigned to show around prospective student Sergey Brin. According to both, they disagreed about nearly everything during that first meeting. Yet their intellectual clash sparked a partnership that would change the internet forever.

Their research project, initially called "Backrub" in 1996, analyzed the web's "link structure" to determine page importance. The algorithm, named PageRank after Larry Page, considered both the number and quality of links to a page‚Äîa revolutionary approach that delivered far superior results to existing search engines.

By 1997, they renamed their search engine "Google," a playful misspelling of "googol" (the number 1 followed by 100 zeros). The name reflected their mission to organize the web's seemingly infinite information.

The turning point came when Sun Microsystems co-founder Andy Bechtolsheim wrote them a $100,000 check made out to "Google Inc."‚Äîbefore the company even existed. This forced them to incorporate, using the garage of Susan Wojcicki (future YouTube CEO) as their first office, complete with a ping-pong table and bright blue carpet.

The 2015 redesign marked Google's most significant visual change‚Äîreplacing its serif typeface with a custom sans-serif font that better reflected its clean, modern approach. The colors were slightly modified to appear more vibrant on digital displays while maintaining the playful, recognizable sequence (blue-red-yellow-blue-green-red).

The Art of Google Doodles

Google Doodles‚Äîthe playful alterations of the Google logo‚Äîbegan in 1998 when Larry and Sergey placed a stick figure drawing behind the second 'o' to indicate their attendance at the Burning Man festival. This whimsical tradition has since evolved into a global phenomenon celebrating:

  • Historical events and anniversaries
  • Scientific breakthroughs
  • Cultural icons and holidays
  • Interactive games and animations

Today, a dedicated team of "Doodlers" creates hundreds of Doodles annually, often featuring sophisticated animations, games, and even AI-powered experiences that engage millions worldwide.

The Alphabet Era & Beyond

In 2015, Google underwent a historic restructuring, creating Alphabet as its parent company. This allowed Google to focus on its core internet products while ambitious "moonshot" projects operated as separate entities:

August 2015

Alphabet Inc. announced as new parent company

December 2019

Sundar Pichai becomes CEO of both Google and Alphabet

2023

Google achieves quantum supremacy with 72-qubit processor

2025

Google Brain merges with DeepMind to form Google DeepMind

Current Leadership: Sundar Pichai, who joined Google in 2004, now leads both Google and Alphabet. Under his leadership, Google has expanded into AI, quantum computing, and health tech while maintaining its core mission to organize the world's information.
