AI 171 Crash Explained

Published on 15th June, 2025

Analysis by Aditya Jha

Important Disclaimer

I am not a flight aviation analyst or expert. I am an educator who has researched this topic extensively. While I strive for accuracy, this analysis represents my understanding based on available information and research. Always refer to official investigation reports for authoritative information.

On June 12, 2025, Air India Flight AI 171, a Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner (registration VT-ANB) crashed just 36 seconds after takeoff from Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport in Ahmedabad, India. The flight was scheduled to travel to London Gatwick but ended in tragedy when it plunged into the B.J. Medical College hostel building in Meghani Nagar, a residential area near the airport.

Casualty Statistics

Onboard: 242 people (230 passengers + 12 crew) - Only 1 survivor

On Ground: 39-50 fatalities, over 60 injured

Total Death Toll: Approximately 280 people

This marks the first fatal hull-loss involving a Boeing 787 Dreamliner since its introduction in 2011.

Possible Causes of the Crash

Based on preliminary reports and my research, here are the most likely factors that contributed to this tragic accident:

  • 1. Engine Failure

    The aircraft's General Electric GEnx engines reportedly experienced a complete loss of thrust shortly after takeoff. The Ram Air Turbine (RAT) deployed automatically, indicating possible dual-engine failure. The 787's "more-electric" architecture may have complicated the power situation.

  • 2. Flaps and Slats Configuration

    Initial reports suggest the flaps and slats might not have been in the correct position for takeoff, potentially affecting lift. The aircraft reached 625 meters but then began an uncontrolled descent.

  • 3. Wheels Causing Excessive Drag

    Evidence suggests the landing gear remained extended after takeoff, creating significant air resistance. This would explain the rapid loss of altitude despite the nose-up attitude maintained by pilots.

  • 4. Technological Limitations

    The 787's advanced systems may have contributed to the crash through:

    • Possible electrical system faults in the "more-electric" architecture
    • Software issues in the flight control computers
    • Automated systems preventing manual override in critical situations

  • 5. Possible Negligence Factors

    While investigation is ongoing, potential human factors include:

    • Pre-flight checklist not properly completed
    • Weight and balance calculations errors
    • Inadequate response to initial warnings

The Sole Survivor: Vishwash Kumar Ramesh

The only survivor was Vishwash Kumar Ramesh, a 40-year-old British national of Indian origin seated in 11A near an emergency exit.

Why he survived:

  • His section of fuselage detached and came to rest relatively intact
  • An emergency exit door broke open, providing escape route
  • Despite injuries and burns, he managed to remove seatbelt and exit
  • Was hospitalized in Ahmedabad but recovered within 6 days

Ramesh later carried his younger brother's coffin at the funeral, a poignant moment captured in viral footage.

Investigation Updates

The official investigation is being conducted by India's Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau (AAIB) with support from the NTSB and Boeing:

  • Both black boxes (CVR and FDR) recovered, though damaged
  • Preliminary report expected within 30 days
  • Focus areas include engine performance, flight control systems, and maintenance records
  • Air India (owned by Tata Sons) reports no prior maintenance issues with the aircraft

Lessons and Safety Improvements

This tragedy may lead to several aviation safety enhancements:

  1. Revised Takeoff Procedures: Additional checks for flap/slat configuration and landing gear retraction
  2. Engine Design Changes: Potential modifications to the GEnx engines to prevent similar failures
  3. Flight Control Software Updates: Changes to allow more pilot override capability in emergencies
  4. Airport Safety Zones: Reevaluation of residential areas near airport flight paths
  5. Emergency Training: Enhanced pilot training for dual-engine failure scenarios during takeoff
